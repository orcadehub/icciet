Author Guidelines

Submission Policy
  • Only original research papers are accepted.
  • Review papers and short notes are not eligible.
  • Papers must follow the conference's prescribed format.
  • Non-compliant papers will not be considered for review.
  • Multiple submissions are strictly prohibited.
  • Papers must not be published or submitted elsewhere.
Plagiarism Policy
  • Papers with less than 13% plagiarism pass to review.
  • 21-30% plagiarism: returned for reduction and resubmission.
  • 31%+ plagiarism: rejected outright.
  • Plagiarism check excludes references and biographies.
Review Policy
  • Papers undergo single-blind review by at least two independent experts.
  • Reports submitted within the review timeline.
  • TPC oversees the process; reviewers receive certificates.
  • Reviewers must be research scholars or PhD holders.
Acceptance Policy
  • Papers must meet criteria for quality, novelty, research objectives, language, presentation, validation, analysis, and references.
  • Acceptance communicated via CMT system.
  • Minor revisions may be requested before final acceptance.
  • Authors of rejected papers are notified immediately.
  • Final decision by the track's dedicated committee.
Registration Policy
  • At least one author must register for accepted papers.
  • Registration is required for presentation and proceedings inclusion.
  • Fee includes kit, presentation, certificate, and proceedings.
  • Fee varies by track; detailed info via registration desk.
  • Payment and details sent in acceptance letter.
Presentation Policy
  • All registered authors present a 15-min PowerPoint in offline mode.
  • Limited online presentations may be allowed under valid reasons, upon permission.
  • Presentations: 12 min talk + 3 min Q&A.
  • Prepare concise PowerPoint to fit the time.
Malpractice Statement

ICCIET'25 upholds the highest standards of academic integrity. Plagiarism, data falsification, redundant publication, or unethical behavior are strictly prohibited. All parties must adhere to ethical guidelines and maintain transparency, originality, and honesty. Suspected malpractice will be investigated and may lead to exclusion or retraction.

Selection Criteria
  • Relevance to conference theme.
  • Originality & Innovation in research or application.
  • Academic Rigor: sound objectives, methodology, and findings.
  • Clarity & Structure in writing and organization.
  • Impact & Contribution to the field or ongoing advances.
Rejection Criteria
  • Non-alignment with conference themes.
  • Plagiarism or ethical violations.
  • Lack of originality or recycled work without new contributions.
  • Poor quality, insufficient detail, or weak methodology.
  • Failure to meet submission/formating guidelines.
